Temples of Thailand

Wat Arun (The Temple of Dawn)

Wat Arun is one of Bangkok's most iconic landmarks, known for its stunning riverside location and intricate design. The central prang (spire) is decorated with colorful porcelain and seashells, creating a dazzling effect when the sun rises and sets.

Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha)

Located within the Grand Palace grounds, Wat Phra Kaew is regarded as the most sacred Buddhist temple in Thailand. It houses the Emerald Buddha, a potent religio-political symbol and the palladium of Thai society.

Wat Rong Khun (The White Temple)

Wat Rong Khun, located in Chiang Rai, is a modern Buddhist temple renowned for its striking white exterior and intricate architectural details. Designed by Thai artist Chalermchai Kositpipat, the temple symbolizes the purity of Buddha and the path to enlightenment. Its stunning murals and sculptures combine traditional Thai art with contemporary symbolism, making it both a spiritual site and an artistic landmark.
