Festivals of Thailand

Songkran Festival (Thai New Year)

Songkran is the most famous festival in Thailand, celebrating the Thai New Year in April. It is known worldwide for its massive water fights, symbolizing the washing away of bad luck and sins. It is also a time for family reunions and temple visits.

Loy Krathong (Festival of Lights)

Celebrated on the full moon of the 12th lunar month, Loy Krathong involves releasing decorated baskets (krathongs) onto rivers to pay respect to the water spirits. The sight of thousands of flickering lights on the water is truly magical.

Bun Bang Fai (Rocket Festival)

Bun Bang Fai is a traditional festival celebrated mainly in northeastern Thailand (Isan). It marks the beginning of the rainy season and is intended to encourage rainfall for a good harvest. Villagers craft homemade rockets, decorate them, and launch them into the sky during lively parades accompanied by music, dancing, and colorful celebrations. The festival reflects local beliefs, community spirit, and Thai cultural heritage.
